### Observed Problem

Small Italian wine producers — the backbone of Italy's prized DOC and DOCG appellations — are being outpaced by climate change at precisely the moment they are least equipped to respond. Irregular ripening windows, late-season heat spikes, and unpredictable frost events are compressing the viable harvest window by an estimated 12 days per decade in Southern Italy. Yet the precision agriculture platforms that could help — satellite-guided irrigation management, AI-driven phenology forecasting, soil microbiome analytics — are designed for and priced at industrial operators managing hundreds of hectares.

The small producer has no meaningful access to these tools. They rely on generational intuition, weather apps, and informal networks. When the climate shifts faster than intuition can adapt, they lose — quietly, without fanfare, and without the data to understand why.

## Narrow Problem Statement

> **Small-scale Italian wine producers (< 5 ha, concentrated in Basilicata, Campania, Sicily, and Calabria) are losing 30–45% of annual yield to climate-driven harvest timing failures and post-harvest spoilage, yet have no access to affordable, vineyard-specific predictive intelligence — because every existing precision viticulture platform is cost-structured for industrial agriculture, not artisan producers.**

---

## Customer Segment

- **Who:** Independent wine growers managing 1–5 hectares of indigenous Italian grape varieties (Aglianico, Nero d'Avola, Greco, Fiano, etc.)
- **Where:** Southern Italy — particularly Basilicata, Campania, Sicily, Calabria
- **Context:** Multigenerational family estates; limited capital; deep varietal knowledge; acute climate vulnerability; DOC/DOCG certification at risk
- **Pain:** Cannot afford the tools that could save their harvest and their identity
- **Opportunity:** 270,000 producers × ~€2,400/year affordable subscription = **€648M addressable market in Italy alone**, before Europe-wide expansion
