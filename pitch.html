Lessons Learned

What the journey
taught us.

From a short story about a clownfish in a vineyard to a €648M market opportunity

01

Constraints create innovation

The best products don't emerge from unlimited budgets — they emerge from real constraints. A €280 sensor kit exists because €40,000/year was unconscionable for the people who needed it most.

02

The interface is the product

No amount of AI sophistication matters if the user won't touch it. Meeting Italian farmers in WhatsApp — where they already live — was the single most important design decision we made.

03

Data compounds like interest

Each producer who joins the cooperative data pool makes every other producer's model more accurate. The network effect of agricultural intelligence is a genuine defensible moat.

04

Trust is the hardest technology

Farmers in Southern Italy don't adopt new tools from strangers. The Coldiretti partnership — Italy's most trusted agricultural body — unlocked distribution no amount of marketing spend could have bought.

05

The "unsexy" market is the opportunity

Industrial agri-tech gets the headlines and the VC attention. 270,000 small producers were invisible — not because they weren't valuable, but because no one looked carefully enough.

06

Climate is the tailwind

We don't need to convince anyone that climate is a problem. Every producer in Southern Italy has felt it in their hands. We simply needed to arrive with an answer before another harvest was lost.

07

Narrative unlocks insight

The story of Sofia, a clownfish, and an abandoned Matera vineyard led directly to a €648M market insight. Sometimes the strangest starting point illuminates the clearest path.
