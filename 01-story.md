# Step A — Short Story

**Parameters:** 🇮🇹 Italy · 🍇 Grapes · 🐠 Clownfish · ⛸️ Ice Skating · 🏚️ Abandoned Building

---

In the frost-cracked courtyard of an abandoned masseria outside Matera, where a wild grapevine had long since swallowed the crumbling limestone walls and sent its roots through every forgotten crack, a marine biologist named Sofia found something impossible: a lone clownfish, Arancio, alive in a century-old stone cistern, fed by rainwater filtered through layers of grape leaves and mineral sediment, as if the ruin itself had decided to become an aquarium. She had come to the masseria chasing a childhood memory — her grandmother had once strapped ice skates to her feet right here, in what had been a famous open-air rink that froze each January when the valley air dropped below zero — and now Sofia stood in the same courtyard, the rink long gone, the ice replaced by cracked marble and creeping vines, watching an orange-and-white fish circle a stone bowl as though it had always belonged to the Italian countryside. In that moment, staring at Arancio darting between grape tendrils in water that tasted faintly of harvest, Sofia understood something she would spend the next decade trying to prove: that abandoned places do not simply die — they transform, they adapt, they become something stranger and more alive than whatever came before.
